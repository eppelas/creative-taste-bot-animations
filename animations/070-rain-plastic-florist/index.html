Rain Plastic Florist

A story-driven florist workroom built from the strongest May 11 human correction: one believable worker, translucent strip curtains, wet buckets, and bouquet tasks carry the style instead of another plain portrait, control sheet, or creature ledger.

Scene rule

The person never turns into a portrait target. The mood comes from watery surfaces, suspended bouquets, manual repetition, and the focus logic sliding across one narrow nocturnal room.

Task cycle

01 Receive

Buckets collect rain-chilled stems from the dock.

02 Strip

Plastic curtains shed droplets and reset the aisle glow.

03 Bind

Bench flashes tighten thread and nudge bouquet sway.

04 Cool

Finished bunches dim into the shelf mist before exit.

Idea

Shift the human lane away from portrait ambition and toward one narrow nocturnal task world where wet flowers, plastic curtains, and reflective surfaces do the expressive work.

Interaction

Pointer movement acts like rack focus plus weather bias: nearby zones sharpen, brighten, and attract the worker’s attention while distant areas soften into rain and reflection.

Next

Grow this into a larger florist-night economy with deliveries, ledger tags, cooler rooms, and more believable multi-person task choreography without turning it into a dashboard.
