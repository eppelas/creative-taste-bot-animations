Interaction

Move across the burrow to shift attention, then press or drag over a creature to pet it. Slow touch makes the nearest body squash open, blush, and sing softly while neighbors lean closer and trade warmth. Quick pokes still work, but they make the local nest tense first.

Idea

A browser-native tactile habitat where several awkward soft creatures live together inside one warm burrow, keeping the cute branch social and atmospheric instead of mascot-like.

Interaction

Pointer movement shifts nearby gaze and breath. Pressing or dragging directly over a body creates literal petting: squash, blush, eye changes, bedding compression, and neighbor response all happen in the same scene.

Next

Grow this into a fuller social world: clearer individual personalities, sleep schedules, nest tools, and small rituals so the burrow can feed future landing-page or illustration storytelling instead of remaining one isolated study.
