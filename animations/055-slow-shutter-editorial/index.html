Direction

This page keeps the human lane but avoids another beige modular sheet. The palette shifts back into night glass, reflected street light, and half-legible editorial framing instead of clean app chrome.

Interaction

Pointer movement behaves like rack focus plus wet-glass shear: the nearest frame becomes clearer while the others slip into doubled exposure. Click adds one quick flash pulse rather than a control widget.

Signal Bands

Cyan marks carry the clearest focus law.
Rose marks hold reflected city bleed.
Amber marks track long-shutter body arcs.

Idea

Build a design-usable human editorial study that still feels code native: three figures share one responsive optical chamber instead of becoming either generic fashion photos or another illustrated creature world.

Interaction

The viewer does not operate a panel. They disturb the focus plane directly, causing nearby reflections, window borders, and motion traces to lean toward the cursor and briefly flare on click.

Next

A stronger follow-up could deepen this into a whole nocturnal editorial contact sheet with more bodies, sharper textile logic, or reflections that behave like passing traffic instead of static rain.
